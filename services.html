Individual Counselling · Ages 15 & Up

Personalized support
for your unique needs

Whether you are navigating a specific challenge or simply want to feel and function better, individual therapy provides a dedicated space to focus entirely on you. Sessions are tailored to your concerns, your goals, and your pace.

Sylvia provides individual psychotherapy to individuals from 15 years of age through to seniors, across a wide range of concerns and life transitions.

Our Approach

There is no one-size-fits-all approach when it comes to supporting your therapeutic goals. You can expect your therapist to draw on a variety of evidence-based approaches — including CBT, ACT, EFFT, Solution-Focused Therapy, Psychodynamic approaches, and behavioural methods — selecting what the research shows works best for your situation.

As with any relationship, we all need to feel safe and connected with those we open up with. The therapeutic relationship is central to your counselling journey and your therapeutic gain.

Areas Commonly Addressed
  • Anxiety, Depression & Emotional Regulation
  • ADHD, ASD & Behavioural Concerns
  • Grief, Loss & Life Transitions
  • Burnout & Stress Management
  • Self-Esteem, Shame & Self-Identity
  • Interpersonal & Communication Skills
  • Chronic Illness & Health Concerns
  • Ruptured Relationships & Loneliness

Parent Counselling

Parenting does not come
with a manual — and that's okay

Parenting is one of the most important roles we can embrace in life. Yet there is no reliable handbook for navigating its ups and downs. How we parent is shaped by our values, beliefs, cultural influences, personal strengths, and the individual needs of our children.

Parent counselling supports you in expanding your "toolbox" with knowledge, guidance, and strategies that align with your own parenting values. Together, we explore how to move from feeling overwhelmed and burnt out to feeling comfortable, confident, and capable.

Support is available individually or as a couple.

A Personal Note from Sylvia

As a parent myself, I know how important it is to feel confident and comfortable with how you choose to parent. In our sessions, you can expect to feel supported as we explore your challenges and discover new strategies together — without bias or judgment.

What to Expect in Sessions

  • Explore parenting values, beliefs, and family dynamics
  • Develop insight into current challenges and concerns
  • Identify personal and family goals
  • Establish appropriate expectations and boundaries
  • Understand the causes of behaviours and your role within them
  • Improve understanding of triggers and preventative measures
  • Learn how to connect, validate, and redirect effectively
  • Receive psychoeducation on the functions of behaviour

Common Therapeutic Gains

Improved parent-child relationship
Better communication and understanding
Rebuilt trust and connection
Greater knowledge of mental health
Support for healthy life changes
Increased emotional regulation
Family members feel valued and respected
Increased confidence and self-identity

Family Counselling

Supporting the whole family,
together

Family dynamics are complex, and the challenges one member faces ripple through everyone. Family counselling creates a shared space where all voices are heard and each person is valued.

Whether you are navigating communication breakdowns, rebuilding connection after conflict, supporting a child through difficulty, or adjusting to a significant life change — family therapy helps the whole unit find a path forward together.

Sessions are grounded in evidence-based practice and designed to meet your family where it is, not where it is expected to be.

What We Work Towards

Improved communication, rebuilt trust, and a stronger sense of connection are at the heart of family counselling. Each session is collaborative, respectful, and tailored to the values and needs of your unique family unit.

Areas Commonly Addressed
  • Communication breakdowns and conflict
  • Behavioural challenges in children and teens
  • Ruptured or strained family relationships
  • Life transitions affecting the family unit
  • Parenting differences and co-parenting support
  • Supporting a family member with mental health concerns
  • Grief, loss, and navigating difficult diagnoses together
